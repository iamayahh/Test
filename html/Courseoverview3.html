STEPS OF MAKING CANDLES

Step 1: Calculate Wax and Fragrance Weight

Determine the total amount of wax and fragrance needed
based on the container size. Fill the container with water,
then weigh it to calculate the required volume. Choose a
fragrance ratio between 7% and 12%, depending
on your preference.

Step 2: Prepare Wax Base and Fragrance

Weigh the wax and fragrance separately. Melt
the wax using a double boiler. Once the wax reaches
a temperature of 55°C (131°F), add the fragrance
and stir thoroughly to ensure a smooth mixture.

Step 3: Insert the Wick

Use pre-waxed wicks and secure them
at the bottom of the container using a small amount
of melted wax or glue. Ensure the wick is centered
with the help of a wick holder or clamp.

Step 4: Pour the Wax

Once the wick is secured, carefully
pour the wax mixture into the container and
allow it to cool gradually.

Step 5: Cooling

Let the candle cool completely until
it solidifies. After it has fully cooled, trim the wick
to about 8-10mm (0.3-0.4 inches).

Final Step: Curing

Curing is essential for maximizing
the candle's scent throw. Allow the
candle to rest for at least 2 days to achieve
its full fragrance potential.
